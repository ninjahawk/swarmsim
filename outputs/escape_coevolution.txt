Two-sided co-evolution: prey escape weight (capture/removal) vs predator lead_time (capture success)
  N=150  n_pred=6  400 tu co-evolution  predator selection every 20 tu  3 seeds
  Predators start at RANDOM lead times in [0,5]; F66 found lead~2 most disruptive by hand.

Exp0 -- predator-only (prey FROZEN at w=0): does capture-selection find the F66 disruption optimum (~2)?
  frozen : predator lead 2.35 -> 3.03+/-0.31   prey mean w_end=0.00 (escaper frac 0.00)   Phi_end=1.00

Exp1 -- full co-evolution, no-escape prey start (w0=0): asymmetry test.
  co w0=0: predator lead 2.35 -> 3.21+/-1.50   prey mean w_end=0.70 (escaper frac 0.47)   Phi_end=0.80

Exp2 -- full co-evolution, seeded escaper prey (f=0.5): can any evolved lead recover capture?
  co seed: predator lead 2.35 -> 2.27+/-2.26   prey mean w_end=2.00 (escaper frac 0.99)   Phi_end=1.00

  -> Q1: against FROZEN no-escape prey the predator lead evolves to ~3.0 tu. Capture-selection
     optimises for CAPTURES, which need NOT match the F66 most-DISRUPTIVE value (~2 tu): the lead that catches
     the most prey and the lead that most reduces coherence are NOT the same objective.
  -> Q2 (arms-race ASYMMETRY): from no escape, prey stay low (w~0.70, the F87 origination brake) so
     the predator wins by optimising lead; from seeded escape, escape dominates (w~2.00) and the
     predator cannot recover -- its lead trait even DECAYS to ~2.3 under relaxed selection (no
     captures to select on). The race is ASYMMETRIC: the predator optimises freely from any start,
     the prey counter is origination-limited, so de-novo co-evolution favours the predator and the
     prey only win when escape is already present (F70/F87/F88). 2-3 seed noise; signs are robust.
