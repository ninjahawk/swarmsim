2026-05-10 1.25 Encirclement predator strategy. Each predator assigned a fixed compass angle; chases CoM + R_enc*(cos(theta), sin(theta)) so they approach from equally spaced directions. Three experiments: radius sweep (R_enc=0..0.25, n_pred=3), encirclement vs naive (n_pred=1..4, R_enc=0.15), flock-breaking threshold (n_pred=1..8, R_enc=0.15). Key result: at n_pred=6, Phi=0.769 -- the first substantial coherence reduction across all strategies tested. Naive and coordinated predators never exceeded this. Optimal radius is R_enc~0.15. Min pred-prey distance falls to 0.050 vs 0.105 for naive. Added Finding 14.
2026-05-10 2.00 Encirclement scaling and fragmentation analysis. (1) Scaling: tested whether the n_pred=6 encirclement threshold scales with flock size N. Fixed n_pred=6 across N=50-500 shows larger flocks resist better (dilution). Fixed ratio 6/350 applied to larger N shows disruption gets WORSE -- at N=500, n_pred=9 gives Phi=0.654, lowest in all experiments. Both N=100 and N=350 converge to Phi~0.67 at n_pred=10-12 regardless of flock size. No simple scaling law exists. (2) Fragmentation: when Phi drops to 0.77 under encirclement, flock does NOT dissolve. Predators compress it spatially (60 clusters naive -> 24 clusters encircle) while splitting it directionally -- each sub-flock escapes toward a gap between predators and remains internally coherent (largest_phi=0.997). This is flock division, not dissolution. Analogous to wolf-pack herding. Added Findings 15 and 16. Wrote panic.py and predator_sensing.py for next sessions. Read textbook chapter and compared project scope to book exercises.
